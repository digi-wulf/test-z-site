Norma Zenteno was a talented and beloved Houston singer, songwriter and musician. She was the oldest daughter of Roberto Zenteno, a big band leader and trumpet player who came to Houston in the 1950â€™s and performed at venues all over the city until his death in 2004. Norma was a natural talent and joined her father's band while still a teenager.

Her family, friends and fans miss her dearly but her musical legacy continues. In remembrance of Norma Zenteno, and in honor of her life and legacy, there will be a statue placed in her honor in Houston's East End, the historic community where she grew up and where she and her family lived for many years.
